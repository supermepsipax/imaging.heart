# IMG-Heart

Background:
The coronary arteries are the vessels that supply oxygenated blood to the heart. While there is a
general, textbook definition of their underlying anatomy together with individual segment definitions,
there is also a wide variation in the actual physical anatomy between individual subjects. These
variations can be a contributing factor in the development of diseases like atherosclerosis (narrowing
of vessels and blood flow restriction due to plaque buildup in the vessel walls). For example, features
such as sharp bifurcation angles, high tortuousity or short side branches have been associated with
disturbed blood flow and increased likelihood of atherosclerotic plaque buildup. A better
understanding and quantification of such coronary anatomy features may therefore help in identifying
individuals at higher risk for disease.

Project description:
The main task of this project is to develop and implement a number of local vessel anatomy
descriptors based on real patient data (CT scans and corresponding segmented masks). Example
descriptors include bifurcation angles and hierarchy, branch lengths, diameter variation, curvature and
tortuosity. The descriptors are then combined to form a distinct patient-specific anatomical profile. The
profiles can then be compared across patients to explore variability and potential patterns. If time
permits, the extracted anatomical profiles can be used in basic exploratory statistical analysis or as
training data for classification, to try and detect the profiles with higher risk of disease
(atherosclerosis).
